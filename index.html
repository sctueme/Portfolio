Complexity vs Insufficient Data

An experiment to see how far can model complexity help in classifying beer styles based on recipes that contain very general information (like color, alcohol content, etc). As expected, due to the lack of apparent separability of the data and the fact that the variables are not specific enough to differentiate beer styles, the results show that using increasingly complex methods with hyperparameters optimized with three different libraries can not compensate for the lack of important data. A dendrogram is built to attempt to visualize better the similarities between each beer style.

Wearable Device Data for Predicting Physical Activity States

A successful attempt to replicate (and in some aspects outperform) the results of the research paper https://www.researchsquare.com/article/rs-17022/v1 where the aim is to classify the level of physical activity (or activity state) of participants with measurements taken from Apple Watch & Fitbit data.
